How to Review a Match

Don’t just watch your VOD and think, “I messed up.” Use a repeatable process so you actually learn. Fill this out for important sets (tournaments, close losses, or matches vs strong players).

  1. Write down the basic info: opponent, character, stage, and result.
  2. Watch the set once without pausing.
  3. On the second watch, pause during big momentum swings or mistakes.
  4. Answer the questions below for neutral, advantage, and disadvantage.
  5. Pick 1–2 specific habits to fix in your next practice session.

Match Info

Player / Opponent

Your Tag: __________________________

Opponent Tag: ______________________

Their Character(s): ________________

Event Details

Event / Bracket: ____________________

Result (e.g., 2–1 L, 2–0 W): ________

Stages Played: _____________________

Neutral Game

1. How did most hits start for YOU?

Examples: whiff punishes, landing aerials, grabs, hard reads.

2. How did most hits start for THEM?

Were you running in too much, jumping from the corner, or getting hit by projectiles?

3. What was one bad habit in neutral you noticed?

(“I always dash in after shielding a projectile,” “I kept jumping at them from too far away.”)

Advantage (Ledge, Juggles, Offstage)

4. When you got them at ledge, did you keep them there?

Did you cover rolls, jumps, and neutral get-ups, or did they escape for free?

5. Did you drop juggles by overcommitting?

Were you swinging too much instead of repositioning underneath them?

6. Did you have chances to edgeguard but backed off?

Write one specific situation where you could have gone offstage safely.

Disadvantage (Juggles, Corner, Offstage)

7. How did you usually try to land?

Did you mix fastfall, drift, airdodges, and attacks, or repeat one option?

8. When you were cornered, what did you do?

Did you roll in, jump, or press buttons on shield? Did they punish this?

9. Offstage, were you predictable?

Did you recover high/low, early/late, and sometimes attack from ledge to reset?

Summary & Next Steps

10. One thing you did well this set:

Give yourself credit for something specific.

11. One habit you will work on in the next week:

Connect this to a drill on the Drills page (e.g., ledge trapping, disadvantage escape).

12. If you played this set again right now, what would you change first?

Good players watch VODs. Great players use VODs to change specific habits.
