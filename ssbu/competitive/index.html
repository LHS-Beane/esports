Tournament Mindset & Game Plan

Being “good at friendlies” is not the same as being tournament ready. This page helps you build a repeatable process for picks/bans, game 1 plans, stock trading, and mental resets.

Before the Set

  • • Breathe and slow your thoughts.
  • • Decide your starter stage & character.
  • • Choose 1–2 goals (e.g., “ledge trap better”).

During the Set

  • • Track their habits at ledge & in disadvantage.
  • • Trade stocks smartly—don’t panic offstage.
  • • Stay aware of percent kill ranges.

After the Set

  • • Write down 1 thing you did well.
  • • Write down 1 thing to practice later.
  • • If you lost, cool down before friendlies.

Stage & Counterpick Planning

Every character has stages that fit their game plan and stages that feel uncomfortable. Build a simple “pick/ban” game plan so you’re never guessing at the setup screen.

Your Stage Notes

For your main, list:

  • 2–3 favorite starter stages and why.
  • 1–2 “comfort” counterpicks that fit your win condition.
  • 1–2 stages you usually want to ban.

Example: “Mario – Loves triplats for combo ladders, bans big flat stages vs zoners.”

Opponent Stage Habits

Pay attention to where certain archetypes like to go:

  • Swordies: strong on wide stages with room (PS2, Town & City).
  • Zoners: like big maps for space (Kalos, FD).
  • Combo characters: prefer platforms to extend strings.

Use this to guide bans even if you don’t know their exact character matchup.

Stock Trading & Risk Management

“Stock trading” is about understanding when it’s worth taking a risk to close a stock, and when you need to back off and keep your lead safe.

When to Push

  • You are behind a full stock and need a momentum shift.
  • Opponent is offstage with no jump.
  • You’ve identified a strong habit (e.g., always jump from ledge).

These are moments where a bigger edgeguard or read can be reasonable.

When to Chill

  • You are up a full stock or more.
  • You’re at kill percent and they’re at low percent.
  • You’re in the corner and they are pressuring safely.

Back up, reset center stage, and force them to take the first risk.

Pre-Match Checklist

Use this before each set to lock in and avoid playing on autopilot.

  1. Deep breath in for 4 seconds, out for 4 seconds.
  2. Decide which character you’re starting with.
  3. Know your preferred starter stage (and what you want to ban).
  4. Pick 1–2 focus goals (e.g., “ledge trap more patiently,” “respect their out-of-shield options”).
  5. Remind yourself: you are here to learn, not just to win right now.

Use this page before and after sets. Consistent process leads to consistent results.
