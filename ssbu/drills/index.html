How to Use These Drills

Don’t just queue into online and hope to get better. Use these drills with a clear focus. Most can be done either with a partner or by using training mode + CPU.

  • Pick 2–4 drills per session.
  • Run each for 5–10 minutes with real intention.
  • Track your progress (success rate, time, consistency).

1. Neutral Game Drills

Dash–Shield–Dash Cycling

Goal: Improve movement fluidity & defense.

Move back and forth across the stage using dash → shield → dash → spot-dodge → dash. Keep a steady rhythm while staying ready to punish.

Success: 60 seconds without dropping the pattern or mis-inputting.

Whiff Punish Practice

Goal: Punish missed attacks consistently.

Partner uses safe but spaced aerials/tilts. Your job is to stay just out of range and punish any move that whiffs using dash attack, grab, or your main combo starter.

Success: 7+ clean whiff punishes in 60 seconds.

Safe Aerial Spacing Reps

Goal: Land aerials safely on shield.

Practice short hop → fastfall aerial on an opponent holding shield. Focus on hitting low to the ground and drifting back or down-tilt/spot-dodge after.

Success: 20 aerials in a row without getting shield grabbed.

Burst Range Awareness

Goal: Understand when you are in danger.

Stand just outside your opponent’s dash attack/grab range. Partner tries to hit you with a burst option; you focus only on micro-adjusting in and out of that threat range.

Success: Survive 30 seconds without being hit.

2. Ledge Trapping & Advantage

Ledge Option Rotation

Partner hangs on ledge and chooses one option: neutral get-up, roll, jump, or get-up attack. You stand at roll distance and try to cover their option with your character’s best tools.

  • Run 20 reps, then switch roles.
  • Track how many you correctly cover.

2-Frame Practice

Have opponent recover to ledge with a consistent timing. Use down tilt, f-tilt, or down smash to try to hit them right before ledge snap (2-frame).

Goal: Land at least 5 2-frames in 5 minutes.

No-Landing Juggle Drill

Launch your partner upward and try to keep them in the air as long as possible using up-airs and repositioning. Partner may drift, fastfall, and airdodge, but not attack.

Goal: Keep them airborne for 6–10 seconds repeatedly.

Platform Extension Strings

On Battlefield/PS2, start a juggle, then specifically route into platform extensions (land on platform, jump again, continue juggles). Focus on smooth movement, not just spamming aerials.

Goal: 3+ hit juggles with at least one platform extension.

3. Offstage & Disadvantage

Offstage Intercept Drill

Both players jump offstage together. The “aggressor” must intercept using fair/nair/bair, while the “defender” tries to mix drift and airdodge to make it back.

Goal: Aggressor gets 60%+ successful edgeguards; defender survives 40%.

No Double Jump Recovery

Start offstage without your double jump. Practice mixing low/high recoveries, early/late up-B, and aggressive recoveries that hit the opponent.

Goal: Recover successfully 8/10 times vs light pressure.

Disadvantage Mix-Up Drill

Partner juggles you while you rotate through four options: fastfall, drift back, neutral airdodge, directional airdodge. You are not trying to hit them, only to escape.

Goal: Escape juggle within 3–4 interactions.

Rising Aerial Edgeguard

Run offstage, use a rising aerial (fair/nair/bair) to hit the recovering opponent, then drift back and recover safely. Focus on spacing and survivability.

Goal: Land 5 successful rising edgeguards in a row without SDs.

4. Kill Confirms & Combos

In training mode, set CPU to “Random DI” and practice your character’s main confirms:

  • 1–2 combo starters at low % (bread-and-butter).
  • 1 mid-percent combo that leads to ledge or juggle.
  • 1–2 kill confirms (jab → back air, nair → up smash, etc.).

Goal: Land each confirm 10 times in a row without dropping input/timing.

5. Movement & Tech Skill

Short Hop / Fastfall Drill

Do 20 perfect short hops in a row, then 20 short hop → fastfalls. Focus on consistent height and timing.

If you mess up, reset the count.

Dash Dance & Foxtrot Control

Dash dance between two stage markers (e.g., platforms or ledge to center) while staying ready to punish a phantom enemy. Don’t just wiggle — imagine reactable options.

Goal: 60 seconds of clean, controlled movement.

Use these drills 3–5 days per week. Small, focused sessions beat long, mindless ones.
